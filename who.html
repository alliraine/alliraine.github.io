who am i

this is a post taken from an old blog written before system discovery. it's being reposted to preserve this writing and our thoughts and feelings at the time
note: this was written 9 months into transition and originally published September 10th, 2022
I've been on hormones for 9 months. I've been out publicly for just about the same. Over the past month or so I've felt lost. Not lost in my gender, not lost in the sense of spirituality, but lost as in who I am. Most people have a journey of self discovery as a teenager. You grow up and discover the harsh truths of reality and feel your first few crushing blows of life, with more or less training wheels on. I know that isn't the case for everyone, however, that's the way things are supposed to go, atleast for some people. I unfortunately was not one of those people.

I grew up in a strong Christian household. I went to church three times a week. I told others that I believed in God, but deep down I never felt anything. I never felt a connection to my religion, never felt like God was moving me, talking to me, or what ever the fuck religious nuts say they feel. I was always taught the same thing, grow up, be a strong husband, find a good wife, settle down, have a family, be the leader of the household, get a good job, work hard, make money, go to church. The shitty part is I believed it. I fell for my parents religious crap just like many other kids have. I let them guide my life, hell my mother even picked my career path for me. It took me years to fully admit I was an atheist, and that was just to myself.

I had a kid at 19 years old. I was married at 18. I love my wife dearly still, and my kids are my world, but I still wonder what would be different if I had a childhood without indoctrination. If I even knew that being trans was possible before my late teens would I have come out earlier? I don't even wish I was cis anymore, I just wish I could have been a happy trans girl as a teenager instead of a depressed, shell of a human in a god damn cult. If my parents had been supportive what the fuck would my life look like right now?

The more I ponder these questions the less answers I have and the more angry, frustrated, and sad I feel. I need to figure out who I am. What parts of me are just left overs from indoctrination, which parts are actually just my parents forming who they wanted me to be, and which parts are actually just... Me?

Life is so different now, I go in public a woman, people perceive me as a woman, I'm expected to be a 26 year old woman. As almost a cruel twist of fate however, I have skipped the training wheels stage of growing up. I have a professional job, I have 3 kids, a wonderful loving wife of 7 years, but all of that doesn't answer my question. I'm sifting through feelings and experiences that most women my age felt and experienced at 13, 14, 15 years old. How in the fuck am I, a grown ass woman, supposed to keep my life together without even being able to answer the simple of question, who am I? How can I process my emotions, bodily changes, and my identity with the world's crushing expectations?

I'm seeking these answers. I hope one day to have them. Unfortunately, this is not that day. Welcome to my blog and welcome to the journey of my personal self discovery.
disclaimer: this site is for us to talk about our life with the world. it's more a less a raw, unfiltered, living memoir of our life, past, present and future. it is most likely riddled with typos, bad sentance structure, and incomplete thoughts. this isn't meant to be a perfect book but a glimps into our life and what it means to be.... us
